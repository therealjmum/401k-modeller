Safe harbor 401(k) vs SIMPLE IRA

Key differences between the two most common small-to-mid company plan structures. A qualified version of either exempts you from state mandates.

Plan type
Safe harbor 401(k)
Best for: owners who want max contributions
Plan type
SIMPLE IRA
Best for: small teams wanting minimal admin
Choose safe harbor 401(k) if...
  • →Owners or HCEs want to defer the full $24,500 without ADP test risk
  • →You want to add a profit-sharing layer on top — total up to $70,000/yr per person
  • →You expect to grow beyond 100 employees
  • →Employees may need access to plan loans
  • →You want a vesting schedule on profit-sharing contributions as a retention lever
Choose SIMPLE IRA if...
  • →You have fewer than 100 employees and want minimal paperwork
  • →No TPA or Form 5500 required — lower admin cost than a 401(k)
  • →A 2% non-elective contribution for all employees is acceptable
  • →Lower contribution limits ($16,500 vs $24,500) are fine for your workforce
  • →You're a startup or early-stage company keeping overhead tight
